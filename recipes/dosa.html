Dosa Recipe

Home

Ingredients

  • 1 cup Rice
  • 1/4 cup Urad Dal (split black gram)
  • 1/2 tsp Fenugreek seeds
  • Salt to taste
  • Water as needed
  • Oil or Ghee for cooking

Steps

  1. Rinse the rice, urad dal, and fenugreek seeds separately under cold water until the water runs clear.
  2. Soak the rice and fenugreek seeds together in a bowl with enough water to cover them. In another bowl, soak the urad dal separately. Let them soak for at least 4-6 hours or overnight.
  3. Drain the soaked rice and fenugreek seeds, reserving the water. Similarly, drain the urad dal, reserving its water as well.
  4. Grind the urad dal using a wet grinder or a high-speed blender, adding a little reserved water as needed, until you get a smooth and fluffy batter. Transfer it to a large bowl.
  5. Next, grind the rice and fenugreek seeds in the same way, adding reserved water as needed, until you get a slightly coarse batter. Combine this with the urad dal batter in the large bowl.
  6. Add salt to taste and mix the batter well. The consistency should be similar to pancake batter â€“ not too thick or too runny.
  7. Cover the bowl with a clean cloth and let the batter ferment in a warm place for 8-12 hours or overnight. The batter should rise and become slightly bubbly.
  8. Once fermented, stir the batter gently. If it's too thick, you can add a little water to adjust the consistency.
  9. Heat a non-stick skillet or cast-iron tawa over medium heat. Lightly grease it with oil or ghee.
  10. Pour a ladleful of batter onto the center of the skillet and spread it out in a circular motion to form a thin dosa.
  11. Drizzle a little oil or ghee around the edges of the dosa. Cook until the edges start to lift and the bottom turns golden brown and crisp.
  12. Flip the dosa and cook for another minute if you prefer it slightly crispy on both sides (optional).
  13. Remove the dosa from the skillet and serve hot with chutney, sambar, or your favorite accompaniments.
